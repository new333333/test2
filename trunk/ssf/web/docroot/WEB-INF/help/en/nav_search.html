Search

Type words or quoted phrases into the Quick search box, and either press Enter on your keyboard or click the Search icon.

SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise presents a full-paged, maximized view, and displays a list of links to any item anywhere in SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise that contains all of the words or phrases you specified. When you are finished with this search, if you want to return to the portal page, click the left arrow icon in the upper right corner of the page, or use other navigation tools to go to the page of your choice.

Clicking the Advanced link performs a more refined search. The small down arrow icon next to the Search icon displays links to saved searches. And the Find people, Find places, and Find tags text boxes provide a way to locate specific types of items quickly.

When using the people, places, and tags text boxes, SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise applies the "type to find" feature. As you type, SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise lists items matching what you have typed so far (all items matching "d", then all items matching "di"). If there is more than one panel of choices, you can click the left arrow icons and right arrow icons to review your choices.

Using the Search Results Page

After conducting a search, the search results page provides tools in the left sidebar, and displays search criteria at the top of the right column, which are immediately followed by items found to match the criteria.

In the Saved searches section of the tools sidebar, you can provide a name for the current search and then click Save. Later, in the navigation bar, you can click the down arrow icon (next to the Search icon) and click the linked name of a saved search you wish to conduct again.

The tools sidebar also provides links to the top ranked places and people involved with matching items. With items at the top of the list being most relevant, these lists tell you places where your search topic is most often being discussed and who is most often contributing to the discussion. Finally, the tools sidebar lists tags that have been applied to matched items.

Notice that, as you conduct searches, SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise places links to each search in the Recent places section of the tools sidebar, so you can easily perform a recent search again. However, because SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise saves only a few of your most recently visited places and searches, use Saved searches for ones that you want to make available to you permanently, until you delete it.

Using Advanced Search

Use advanced search if you want to limit your search based on various additional criteria, including the author of the item, one specific workspace or folder, a category of item (only folders or only personal workspaces), a workflow state, an applied tag, an activity date, and more.

You can access the advanced search form from either the navigation bar or from a search results page; after a quick search, you can click Advanced to switch to an advanced search. If you want to include more than the initially provided criteria, click the More options link in the upper-right corner. (Click the Hide options link to hide the additional criteria again.)

If your search requires more than one item of criteria, click the More criteria... button, and SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise provides another drop-down list for your use. Click the X icon next to a criterion drop-down list to remove it from the form.

Restricting Searches to One Place

To restrict searches to one workspace or folder, do the following:

  1. Click the Advanced link.

  2. In the Advanced search form, click the plus sign (+) next to the top workspace (by default, labeled Workspaces), move through the hierarchy, and select the box next to the workspace or folder you wish to search. (Use the plus sign and minus sign icons, and do not click the titles of workspaces and folders.)

  3. If you want SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise to search every place below your selection in the workspace tree, then select the box next to search all sub-places.

  4. Specify any additional advanced search criteria.

  5. Click the Search icon, in the lower right corner of the top portion of the form.

Search String Rules

Here are some of the rules for search strings:

  1. Case Sensitivity: If you specify all lowercase letters, then SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise returns matches in any case ("tree" matches "tree", "Tree", "TReE", and so on). If you specify one or more uppercase letters, SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise returns only exact matches ("Tree" does not match "tree").

  2. More Than One Word: SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise returns items that contain all words in the search string.

  3. Quoted Phrase: SiteScape ICEcore Enterprise returns items that match all words in order. Case sensitivity rules apply as described previously.

  4. Exclusions: To exclude a word (do not return matches for items that contain this word), place a minus sign (-) in front of it. For example, "-tree" excludes all items containing "tree", "Tree", "TREE", and so on.
