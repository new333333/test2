Understanding Kablink Teaming Teams

Generally, a team is a group of people working together to achieve a shared goal. Kablink Teaming teams provide you with a dedicated area in which to collaborate online, and in which to communicate quickly and easily with teammates.

By default, all people using Kablink Teaming can create a team workspace, which provides your team with a dedicated work area. After you create your team workspace, add as many sub-workspaces or folders (discussions, file libraries, wikis, blogs, and so on) as you need for your work.

Kablink Teaming supports a second type of team that can be applied to any workspace or folder. One reason why this team is useful is because it is a quick way to allow a group of people to participate in a place (for example, adding people to a team in a personal folder allows those people to author new entries). Another application is to designate a group of people as serving in a special role in that place (for example, subject-matter experts or moderators). To create teams of this type, the owner of a workspace or folder can use the Team > Add or delete team members menu item to create that team and can use access control settings to grant additional privileges.

Kablink Teaming communication tools allow for quicker use among team members. For example, in a team workspace, if you click the Meet link at the bottom of a workspace or folder, click Team members, and click Start meeting now, then Kablink Teaming initiates an online meeting, using SiteScape ICEcore Conference, without you having to specify team names individually. Membership in a team also facilitates use of the clipboard, IM using SiteScape ICEcore Conference, and e-mail.
