Understanding Kablink Teaming Teams

Generally, a team is a group of people working together to achieve a shared goal. Teaming teams provide you with a dedicated area in which to collaborate online, where you can communicate quickly and easily with teammates.

By default, all people using Teaming can create a team workspace, which provides your team with a dedicated work area. After you create your team workspace, you can add as many sub-workspaces or folders (discussions, file libraries, wikis, blogs, and so on) as you need for your work.

Membership in a team can determine who can view or participate in a workspace or folder. Also, membership in a team can increase the speed of configuration and communication. For example, while working in a team area, you can quickly add all members of a team to a buddy list or to the clipboard.

The following sections provide information on how to manage and work with teams:

Creating a Highly Visible Team Workspace

If you want your team workspace to be highly visible and easy to find in the workspace tree, create the team workspace in the Team Workspaces directory.

  1. Expand Home Workspace in the Workspace Tree Navigation tool, located near the top of the Teaming interface.

    The Home Workspace directory is the highest-level folder in the workspace tree, and might be named something specific to your organization.

  2. Click Team Workspaces.

    If you do not see the option in Step 3, your site administrator has not permitted you to create a new workspace at this level. Continue to expand the workspace tree, following the path of your organizational unit, then click the name of the directory where you want to create the new team workspace. Or, follow the steps in Creating a Team Workspace Inside of an Existing Team Workspace.

  3. Click Add a team workspace.

    The Add a Team Workspace page is displayed.

  4. In the Title section, provide a title for the workspace.

  5. In the Team Members section, specify the team members.

    You can specify individual users, entire groups, or add names that are on your clipboard.

  6. In the Workspace Folders section, select the folders that you want the workspace to contain. If you later decide that you want to add additional folders, you can add them at any time.

  7. (Optional) In the Announcement section, select the checkbox, then type a message in the Announcement Text field.

    This message informs team members about the existence of the workspace, and might also explain the value of the workspace.

  8. Click OK.

The new workspace has now been created.

Creating a Team Workspace Inside of an Existing Team Workspace

Teaming enables you to create nested workspaces, which are workspaces inside of other workspaces. You can create a team workspace inside of any existing workspace where you a have appropriate rights.

You might want to do this if you are not concerned about having high visibility in the workspace tree, and if the team workspace that you want to create consists mostly of members that already belong to a certain workspace.

To create a team workspace inside of an existing workspace:

  1. Navigate to the workspace where you want to create the new team workspace.

  2. Click Manage > Add New Workspace.

    The Add New Workspace page is launched.

  3. In the Workspace Title field, specify a title for the workspace.

  4. In the Workspace section, select Team Workspace.

    You can also create a basic workspace, a project management workspace, and a discussions workspace.

  5. In the Team Members section, specify the team members.

    You can specify individual users, entire groups, or add names that are on your clipboard. You can also click Team Members. This displays a list of all of the members of the team workspace where you are creating your new team workspace. Select the names of the users that you want to include in your new team workspace.

  6. In the Workspace Folders section, select the folders that you want the workspace to contain. If you later decide that you want to add additional folders, you can add them at any time.

  7. (Optional) In the Announcement section, select the checkbox, then type a message in the Announcement Text field.

    This message informs team members about the existence of the workspace, and might also explain the value of the workspace.

  8. Click OK.

The new workspace has now been created.

Communicating Within Teams

Teaming communication tools allow for quicker use among team members. The following communication methods enable you to quickly and efficiently communicate with your team.

Setting Up a Team Meeting

  1. In a team workspace, click Meet in the main sidebar.

  2. Click Team Members, then select the names of the team members with whom you want to meet.

    or

    In the Users field, you can specify individual users with whom you want to meet.

    Also, when you click Clipboard User Names, you can choose from the names that are already on your clipboard.

  3. Click Start Meeting Now, or Schedule for Later, depending on when you want to hold your meeting.

Sending E-Mail to Team Members

  1. Navigate to the desired team workspace.

  2. In the main sidebar, click Team > Send E-Mail To Team.

    or

    In the main sidebar, click Send E-Mail.

  3. In the Recipients section, specify the team members to whom you want to send the e-mail.

  4. In the Message section, compose the e-mail message.

  5. Click OK.

Allowing Non-Team Members to be Visitors

In addition to participating team members, you might want to allow other people to visit your team workspace in a more limited capacity. For example, you can add people to the Visitor role, which restricts them to reading and commenting on existing entries.

To grant visitor access:

  1. Navigate to the team workspace where you want to grant visitor access.

  2. Click Manage > Access Control. Teaming displays the Configure Access Control page.

  3. In the This folder inherits section, ensure that No is selected. If it is not, select No, then click Apply.

  4. Click the Add a User link in the left column of the table, type the first few letters of person's first or last name, and then, in the drop-down list, click the linked name.

    Teaming adds a row to the table for that person.

  5. In the newly created row, select the box in the Visitor column. (If the Visitor column does not exist, click Add a Role > Visitor, located near the top of the table.)

  6. Repeat Step 4 and Step 5 until you have added all desired visitors.

  7. Click the Save Changes button.

  8. Click Close.

Changing the Team Directory Structure to Suit Larger Organizations

This task applies only to site administrators.

By default, people create teams that are sub-workspaces to the Team Workspaces directory. If your organization is large, you might want to create a hierarchy within which people can create their team workspaces. For example, you might want to create sub-workspaces in the team area according to geography (North America, South America, Europe, Africa) or organizational function (Executive, Finance, Marketing, Sales, Human Resources).

Changing the Configuration of the Team Workspaces Directory

To implement a hierarchy of team workspaces, you first need to change the configuration of the Team workspaces directory:

  1. Navigate to and select the Team Workspaces directory.

  2. Click Manage > Configure.

  3. In the Default View section, select Workspace.

  4. Click Apply > Close.

    This removes the Add a Team Workspace button on the Team Workspaces page. Users can no longer create team workspaces at this level in the Workspace tree.

  5. Continue with Creating Sub-Workspaces.

Creating Sub-Workspaces

Sub-workspaces, in the Team Workspaces directory, can be organized by geography (North America, South America, Europe, Africa), organizational function (Executive, Finance, Marketing, Sales, Human Resources), or any other type of organization that you choose.

To create a sub-workspace:

  1. Navigate to the Team Workspaces directory.

  2. Click Manage > Add New Workspace.

  3. In the Workspace Title field, provide a title for the workspace, such as Finance.

  4. In the Workspace section, select Workspace.

  5. Click OK.

  6. In the Team Workspaces directory, select the workspace that you just created.

  7. Click Manage > Configure.

  8. In the Default View section, select Team Workspace Root.

  9. Click Apply > Close.

    The Add a Team Workspace button now appears in the sub-workspace that you just created. Users with appropriate rights can now create workspaces underneath this sub-workspace. This improves the organization of the Team Workspaces directory.

  10. Repeat Step 1 through Step 9 for every sub-workspace that you want to create.

You might want to alter the access control of the sub-workspaces so that people can create their teams only in the appropriate sub-workspaces. Creating group names in advance for organizational sub-groups can greatly facilitate this process.
